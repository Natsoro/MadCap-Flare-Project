Fellahi

Note: Fellahi belly dance, also known as "rural" or "country" belly dance, is a style of belly dance that originated in the countryside of Egypt.

Here are some characteristics of Fellahi belly dance:

Focus on hip and torso movements

Fellahi belly dance emphasizes the use of the hips and torso to create undulating, flowing movements that are both sensual and playful.

Earthy and grounded movements

Fellahi dance is characterized by strong, grounded movements that reflect the rural and agricultural lifestyle of the people who created this style.

 

Incorporation of props

Props such as the cane, basket, and scarf are often used in Fellahi belly dance to add an element of storytelling and playfulness to the performance.

Simplicity and improvisation

Fellahi belly dance is often less choreographed and more improvisational, with dancers using the music as inspiration to create their movements on the spot.
