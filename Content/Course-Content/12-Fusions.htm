Fusions

Belly dance has evolved over time to include various fusion styles such as tribal and ATS. Tribal belly dance is a fusion of traditional Middle Eastern dance with elements of other dance styles, while ATS is a modern group improvisational style that draws inspiration from various cultures. Both styles incorporate unique costuming and props to enhance their performances.

Tribal

Belly dance tribal style is a fusion dance style that blends elements of traditional Middle Eastern dance with other dance forms, including flamenco, Indian classical dance, and contemporary dance.

Some common characteristics of belly dance tribal style include:

  • Group improvisation:
  • In belly dance tribal style, dancers often perform in groups, and the choreography is often improvised. The dancers use cues and gestures to signal to each other and create a cohesive performance.
  • Tribal-inspired costumes: Belly dance tribal style often features costumes that are inspired by traditional tribal clothing, such as full skirts, belts, and headdresses adorned with coins, tassels, and fringe.
  • Strong, grounded movements: Belly dance tribal style emphasizes strong, grounded movements, such as hip drops, shimmies, and undulations. These movements are often performed in isolation, with the torso and hips moving independently.
  • Use of props: Belly dance tribal style often incorporates props such as swords, veils, and zills (finger cymbals) into the performance.
  • Fusion with other dance forms: Belly dance tribal style often incorporates elements of other dance forms, such as flamenco arm movements, Indian classical hand gestures, and modern dance floorwork.
  • Emphasis on community and inclusivity: Belly dance tribal style often emphasizes a sense of community and inclusivity, with dancers of all ages, body types, and backgrounds coming together to celebrate the art form.

ATS

American Tribal Style (ATS) is a style of dance that was created in the 1990s by Carolena Nericcio, founder of the dance troupe FatChanceBellyDance.

The characteristics of ATS include:

  • Group improvisation: ATS is typically performed by a group of dancers who improvise the choreography together in real-time. This means that the dancers need to be highly skilled in reading each other's body language and cues.
  • Use of cues and formations.
  • ATS dancers often wear elaborate costumes that are inspired by tribal cultures from around the world. These costumes typically include layers of flowing fabrics, ornate jewelry, and heavy makeup.
  • ATS emphasizes the connection between the dancers and the audience, as well as the connection between the dancers themselves.
  • While ATS is an improvisational dance style, it also places a strong emphasis on technique and precision. Dancers must have a strong foundation in belly dance technique in order to successfully improvise with a group.
