Classical Egyptian dance

Classical Egyptian dance, oriental, classical, Egyptian, raqs sharqi - a style that cannot be treated as a uniform form of dance with specific rules. Because of the differences in music, we can call different songs classics. They are usually difficult and full of emotions.

They may contain parts (depending on the song) of maksum, folklore such as saidi or khaleegy, taksim (instrument solo), drum solo, etc. In Megance, the dancer can show off a wide range of technical and interpretation skills and knowledge of the rules of matching figures to appropriate rhythms or instruments. It is a dance intended for the stage, marked by great ballet influences.

Traditional songs from Egyptian films of the Golden Era.

Tarab - traditional Arabic songs by famous composers (Atrashe, Wahab), the most popular of which are those performed by Oum Kalthum - the most famous Egyptian singer of all time. The best composers composed for her. The dancer should know exactly the lyrics of the song she is dancing to and interpret them as well as the music. Also for the audience, the reception of the dance to the song Oum Kalthum can be very difficult, Western viewers are not always able to understand it.
